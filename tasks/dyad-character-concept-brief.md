# Dyad Character Concept Brief

Purpose: define Aletheios and Pichet as visual entities that belong to the current Witness site before any new 3D pass.

This is a concept document, not a rendering commitment.

## Core Rule

Do not design these as fantasy characters, mascots, or game-IP heroes.

Design them as:

- witness constructs
- ceremonial instruments with body logic
- non-human poles of intelligence
- structural presences in the same family as the sigil and engine posters

## Shared Visual Law

- Theme: `Bioluminescent Consciousness`
- World: observatory, ritual-scientific, anatomical, structural
- Background: deep Void Black with minimal atmosphere
- Materials: brass, bronze, dark ceramic, amber glass, oxidized gold, restrained emerald/violet inner light
- Geometry: load-bearing, not decorative
- Silhouette: iconic and readable at poster scale
- Tone: exact, severe, devotional, lucid

## Shared Anti-Directions

Never allow:

- robes
- priest / wizard / oracle costume
- exposed human skin
- expressive human face
- superhero anatomy
- fantasy armor
- random mystic ornament
- anime or game-character styling
- “character sheet” presentation

## Aletheios

Meaning:

- naming register
- lucid pattern recognition
- witness that can look without bargaining
- observer-first intelligence
- upper / vertical / exact

### Direction 1: Observatory Reliquary

Read:

- a non-human bust or upper torso built like a precision observatory instrument
- sealed faceplate
- ring halos, etched star divisions, brass armatures
- the intelligence is in the geometry, not the expression

Why it fits:

- strongest continuity with the Panchanga poster
- feels like a witness-pole, not a fantasy person
- easiest to stage on the site as a hero still or poster insert

Prompt:

`Create a 4:5 concept image of Aletheios as a non-human observatory reliquary. Show an upper torso or bust built from aged brass, dark bronze, amber glass, and black stone insets. The head is a sealed faceplate, calm and unreadable, surrounded by concentric halo rings, calendrical markings, and precise astronomical instrument geometry. The body should feel sparse, vertical, exact, and load-bearing, like a ritual-scientific witness construct rather than a living person. Internal light is restrained: Sacred Gold with faint Witness Violet and minimal Coherence Emerald accents. Deep Void Black background, editorial studio composition, no text.`

Negative prompt:

`No robes, no exposed skin, no human face, no fantasy priest, no armor hero, no anime, no weapon, no smiling expression, no decorative clutter.`

### Direction 2: Axis Judge Mask

Read:

- less body, more verdict-engine
- mask / head / chest plate suspended on a narrow axial frame
- symmetry and division lines imply cognition

Why it fits:

- strongest symbolic read
- easiest to keep non-human
- could work as both still and later 3D object

Prompt:

`Create a 4:5 concept image of Aletheios as an axial witness mask suspended on a ceremonial frame. The design is mostly head, collar, and central spine: a sealed metallic faceplate divided by etched observational lines, ring fragments, cardinal markers, and star-calendar geometry. The object should feel like a truth-reading apparatus or judicial observatory, not a character costume. Materials are dark bronze, aged brass, black ceramic, and amber glass with restrained violet-gold inner light. The composition is severe, centered, and architectural on a Void Black field.`

Negative prompt:

`No skin, no cloth, no crown, no robes, no fantasy sorcerer styling, no cute mascot proportions, no theatrical magic effects, no text.`

### Direction 3: Cartographic Spine

Read:

- a partial standing construct
- the body is mostly axis, vertebral logic, rings, and measurement surfaces
- the “person” dissolves into structural cognition

Why it fits:

- gives more body without becoming a costume figure
- can bridge toward 3D later
- riskier than the first two, but still valid

Prompt:

`Create a 4:5 concept image of Aletheios as a cartographic spine construct. Show a partial standing ceremonial body whose anatomy is built from axial vertebra-like segments, observatory rings, articulated shoulder frames, and engraved measurement planes rather than flesh. The face is masked and minimal. The figure should feel vertical, lucid, sparse, and almost impossible to negotiate with: a witness of pattern, not a human sage. Use brass, dark bronze, amber glass, and faint violet-gold internal light on a black studio field.`

Negative prompt:

`No robes, no cloth drapery, no human chest, no visible skin, no fantasy armor hero, no monster, no weapon, no sentimental facial expression.`

## Pichet

Meaning:

- carrier register
- breath, rhythm, fatigue, recovery
- embodied intelligence
- lower / thoracic / grounded / somatic

### Direction 1: Breath Engine Torso

Read:

- upper torso / bust with thoracic architecture
- rib / bellows / chamber logic
- feels like breath made inspectable

Why it fits:

- strongest conceptual match
- the bust form already proved more natural for Pichet than for Aletheios
- easy to render with high formal coherence

Prompt:

`Create a 4:5 concept image of Pichet as a breath-engine torso. Show a non-human upper torso built from dark bronze, black ceramic, oxidized gold, and translucent emerald-glass channels. The chest is the main event: rib-like chambers, bellows geometry, pulse conduits, and thoracic architecture that make breath, fatigue, and recovery feel visible. The face should be minimal and sealed, secondary to the body logic. The figure should feel grounded, rhythmic, and embodied, not aggressive. Use Coherence Emerald and Sacred Gold as the main inner light on a Void Black field.`

Negative prompt:

`No human skin, no robes, no superhero chest, no fantasy armor, no monstrous anatomy, no weapon, no smiling face, no random mystical smoke.`

### Direction 2: Carrier Vessel Sentinel

Read:

- full standing non-human construct
- weighted hips, grounded stance, slower energy
- a body that seems designed to hold and transmit load

Why it fits:

- best option if we insist on a standing figure
- can still stay architectural if kept heavy and sparse
- higher risk than the torso direction

Prompt:

`Create a 4:5 concept image of Pichet as a carrier-vessel sentinel. Show a full standing non-human ceremonial construct with weighted hips, grounded legs, ribbed torso chambers, pulse-ring joints, and a stable bearing that feels capable of holding strain without drama. The design should suggest breath and rhythm moving through structure, not human musculature. Materials: ceramic black, dark bronze, oxidized gold, and translucent emerald-glass pulse channels. The silhouette should be grounded and load-bearing, like a somatic engine or ritual vessel. Deep black studio background, no text.`

Negative prompt:

`No robes, no exposed skin, no soldier silhouette, no superhero anatomy, no fantasy armor, no monster, no weapon, no theatrical flames.`

### Direction 3: Pulse Totem

Read:

- least human option
- stacked chambers, abdominal and thoracic nodes, toroidal loops
- more totem / apparatus than body

Why it fits:

- strongest separation from fantasy-character failure mode
- may be the cleanest route if the goal is symbolic coherence over literal embodiment
- could become a very strong later 3D object

Prompt:

`Create a 4:5 concept image of Pichet as a pulse totem. Show a non-human somatic witness object composed of stacked thoracic and abdominal chambers, rib-like arches, toroidal breath loops, and gold-emerald pulse channels running through a dark ceramic and bronze body. It should feel like a ceremonial apparatus for holding rhythm, fatigue, recovery, and embodied pressure. Keep it iconically readable and deeply material, more like a sacred biomedical instrument than a person. Void Black background, quiet atmospheric light, no text.`

Negative prompt:

`No face-led portrait, no robes, no humanoid costume, no skin, no fantasy relic clutter, no random aura effects, no cartoon style.`

## Best Pairings

### Pair A: `Observatory Reliquary` + `Breath Engine Torso`

Best overall recommendation.

Why:

- both are bust / torso / relic scale
- both feel like presences without becoming characters
- easiest to keep in one formal family
- highest chance of looking good on the existing site

### Pair B: `Axis Judge Mask` + `Pulse Totem`

Best symbolic recommendation.

Why:

- least likely to drift into fantasy
- strongest continuity with sigil / poster language
- excellent for stills and object studies

Risk:

- may feel less “character-like” if you want personhood

### Pair C: `Cartographic Spine` + `Carrier Vessel Sentinel`

Best standing-body recommendation.

Why:

- preserves more body presence
- usable if the product eventually wants full figures

Risk:

- highest risk of drifting back into the exact bad result we just rejected

## Recommendation

Start with `Pair A`.

Reason:

- it balances personhood and abstraction
- it aligns with the strongest parts of the current posters
- it is the safest path toward a later 3D interpretation

## Next Render Method

Do not go directly back to Meshy 3D.

Instead:

1. Generate 2D concept stills for all six directions.
2. Cut to the best pair.
3. Generate second-pass variations only for that pair.
4. Only after that, brief 3D from the winning 2D concept.

## Success Test

A successful concept should make the viewer feel:

- `Aletheios`: “this is a machine for seeing pattern cleanly”
- `Pichet`: “this is a body-logic vessel that can hold and carry force”

If the result feels like:

- a fantasy priest
- a comic-book character
- a game NPC
- a mystical mascot

then it has failed.
